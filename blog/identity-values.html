Strengths and limitations

  • 🍅 logical and analytical
  • 🍅 open to new experiences
  • 🍅 conscientious
  • 🍅 goal oriented

Limitations include:

  • 🌪 jumping into things without much planning
  • 🌪 If I don't understand something, I go down a Google rabbit hole to find out, which is not always productive.
  • 🌪 I tend to try to do everything on my own and not reach out for help when I'm stuck

Explain a situation where you have made an ethical decision. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and how you reflect on the decision now.

My first job out of uni was working as an immigration officer, which involved reviewing NZ residency applications and pushing them forward for approval. The official protocol was for residency applications to be decided on a first-come-first served basis. However some managers disregarded this process and had a tendancy to fast-track application based on the applicant's occupation (For example, people who were nurses, chefs, labourers, etc would have their applications vetoed for any missing documents whereas doctors, CEOs, etc, were given more leniency. In another instance, a manager was not a fan of lawyers, so would be more stringent towards these applicants).

This did not sit right with me, and for months it was difficult to work with these unspoken rules. I questioned this with managers who, realised that this was not a fair process and also screwed up the workflow. Fortunately, it was agreed to for everyone to let go of their bias, and treat every case equally.
