What is Emotional Intelligence?

Emotional intelligence (aka emotional quotient/EQ) is the ability to understand, use, and manage your own emotions in positive ways to relieve stress, communicate effectively, have empathy for others, and build healthy relationships.1

The four core elements of Emotional intelligence are: 2

  • 1. Self-awareness: you recognize your own emotions and how they affect your thoughts and behaviour
  • 2. Self-management: your ability to control impulsive feelings and behaviours
  • 3. Social awareness: having empathy for others.
  • 4. Relationship management: knowing how to develop and maintain good relationships, work well in a team and manage conflict.

How is EQ different to IQ?

The Intelligence Quotient (IQ) is obtained from a standardized test which is designed to assess a person's general intelligence.

IQ respresents abilities such as:

  • - logical reasoning
  • - ability to plan and strategize
  • - abstract thinking
  • - visual and spatial processing

Emotional Quotient (EQ), is the ability to identify and manage your emotions and the emotions of others.

EQ focuses on abilities such as:

  • - identifying emotions
  • - evaluating how others feel
  • - abstract thinking
  • - visual and spatial processing

Why is EQ important?

Research has shown that people with a higher EI are well equipped to work well in teams, deal with change, and manage stress, and enable them to thrive mentally, physically and socially. 3

Emotional Intelligence helps us to:

  • - manage our mental health: regulate our emotions
  • - have greater self-awareness
  • - self-motivate with a focus on achieving internal self-gratification as opposed to external praise or reward
  • - communicate effectively and build stronger relationships
